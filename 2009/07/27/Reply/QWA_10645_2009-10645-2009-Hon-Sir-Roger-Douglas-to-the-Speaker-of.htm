Date:
27 July 2009
Subject
Politics and Government

10645 (2009). Hon Sir Roger Douglas to the Speaker of the House of Representatives (27 Jul 2009): Is he satisfied that the Hon Jim Anderton is entitled to the additional $100,000 he receives to maintain a Leader's office by virtue of the Progressive Party being a registered political party with no less than 500 financial members?
Hon Dr Lockwood Smith (Speaker of the House of Representatives) replied: The statutory provision for the Parliamentary Service to pay leadership funding to each political party is provided for under the Parliamentary Service Act 2000 and the Speaker’s Directions. In the Parliamentary Service Act 2000, a recognised party means a political party for the time being recognised for parliamentary purposes under the Standing Orders of the House of Representatives. In the Speaker’s Directions, a party means a parliamentary party whose members in the House include at least one member. Each parliamentary party receives an allocation for leadership funding. The current allocation is $100,000 (excluding GST) per annum, plus $64,320 (excluding GST) per non-executive member in the caucus. Therefore parties receive an allocation according to the size of their caucus. The Speaker is responsible for approving the level of funding taking into account advice from: • the Appropriations Review Committee and • the Parliamentary Service Commission. The Appropriations Review Committee’s is an independent body made up of up to three persons who cannot be a member of Parliament or an officer or employee of the Parliamentary Service. The Parliamentary Service Commission’s membership includes representatives from every parliamentary party. The current leadership allocation was recommended by the Appropriations Review Committee in its report released in March 2007. After the Appropriations Review Committee released its report, the Parliamentary Service Commission provided advice to the then Speaker as part of the process to make the Speaker’s Directions (1 December 2007). The leadership allocation amounts shown above became available from that date. I am satisfied that the leadership funding entitlement is being applied in accordance with the Parliamentary Service Act 2000 and the Speaker’s Directions.
