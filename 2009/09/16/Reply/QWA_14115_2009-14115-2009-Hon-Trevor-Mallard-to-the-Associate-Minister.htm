14115 (2009). Hon Trevor Mallard to the Associate Minister of Education (16 Sep 2009): What is a list of all committees, working parties or groups, that she has set up since becoming Minister, including the name of the group, date set up, membership names, terms of reference, and reporting date ?
Hon Heather Roy (Associate Minister of Education) replied: Associate Minister of Education Committees, Working Parties and Groups – November 2008 to September 2009 1. The Gifted and Talented Education Reference Group Date Established: 28 August 2009. This group met for one day only. The group comprised representatives from provider groups and associations to consider options for the future support of gifted and talented education. Advice provided by the group is being used to inform the work programme of an ongoing advisory group I intend to set up and that will meet for the first time in October 2009. Membership: Tracy Riley – giftEDnz The Professional Association for Gifted Education Chris Harwood – Ministry of Education Clive Sharpe – Gifted Kids Programme Kate Coleman – Canterbury Association for Gifted Children and Youth Jeanette Christensen – Christchurch Association for Gifted Education Kathy Williams – The Gifted Education Centre Louise Tapper – North Canterbury Support for Gifted and Talented Children Rosemary Cathcart – REACH Education Consultancy Mary St George – The Waikato Association for Gifted Children Rose Blackett - New Zealand Association for Gifted Children Jane Johnston –Wellington Association for Gifted Children Sally Jackson – Minister’s Private Secretary Carolyn Stein – Otago Association for the Gifted and Talented Terms of Reference The purpose of the Gifted and Talented Education Reference Group is to provide advice and guidance to the ministry and government about future directions for provision of support for gifted and talented students in New Zealand across a continuum of approaches. The group comprises named individual representatives of key sector organisations who because of their expertise and experience are able to contribute to this group. This Terms of Reference document sets out the function of the Reference Group. Scope The role of the Reference Group is to provide expert advice on and recommendations for the future direction and organisation of education for gifted and talented children and young people nationally, including: • The principles and practices that should underpin a shared delivery of education for gifted and talented learners. • The design of flexible and responsive models to support gifted and talented learners within and across national and regional networks. Process The Reference Group will meet on 28 August 2009 to develop advice and recommendations for the Ministry and Government. The meeting is hosted by the Associate Minister of Education, Hon Heather Roy. Tracy Riley (Chairperson giftEDnz) and Chris Harwood (Ministry of Education) will co facilitate the meeting. The Ministry of Education will provide a secretariat for the meeting. Confidentiality and Official Information Members of the group will provide free and frank advice in the development of advice and recommendations. Any commercially sensitive information provided to the Reference Group as part of its deliberations will be treated in confidence by the Group. Members will not disclose any confidential information obtained in the course of the development of the advice and recommendations. The Official Information Act 1982 will apply without exception to the activities of the Reference Group. Reporting Date: Not applicable 2. The Advisory Panel on the Review of Special Education. Date established: 19 August 2009 Membership The members of the Advisory Panel have been selected because they bring particular expertise on special education. Members do not sit on the Panel as representatives of any organisation; they are invited as individuals and contribute in that capacity. The members of the Advisory Panel are: • Jill Bevan-Brown, Associate Professor, Special Education, Massey University • Roderick Deane, Economist, Public Sector Reformer and Businessman • Paul Gibson, Senior Disability Advisor, Capital and Coast District Health Board, • Brian Hinchco, Principal, Papatoetoe South Intermediate School, South Auckland • Heather Lear, Parent and Lawyer The Associate Minister may invite other individuals to contribute on an ad hoc basis as issues arise. Terms of Reference Background The Government is undertaking a Review of Special Education. The Review is to examine and make recommendations on a range of issues in special education including access to services, access to schooling, outcomes for students, accountability arrangements, workforce, transitions, and fragmentation of service delivery. There is approximately $450 million invested in special education annually. Government needs to be assured that it is getting best value for this money. The current model of funding to support children and young people with special education needs was developed in the mid-1990s and there has been considerable change in operating environments since that time. Consultation over the last ten years has shown that special education provision is fragmented and not well coordinated. There are also concerns from parents, principals and sector organisations that there is room for improvement on a number of fronts such as improved access and more effective supports. The Terms of Reference for the Review are appended. The Associate Minister of Education, Hon Heather Roy is responsible for the Review and is to establish an Advisory Panel to assist her. Role and scope The Advisory Panel on the Review of Special Education is to provide advice, perspectives and act as a ‘sounding-board’ for the Associate Minister of Education on the range of issues in the Review of Special Education. The Advisory Panel is not a decision-making body. Members will: • provide free and frank advice to the Associate Minister about the issues covered by the Review. • share insights, expertise and experience • provide advice on the alignment between issues in the Review and other programmes and initiatives • provide comment about the Review if requested by the Associate Minister. At the request of the Associate Minister, the Advisory Panel or the Ministry of Education, the Advisory Panel may engage directly with the Ministry of Education on specific matters. Such request will be channelled through the Associate Minister. The Advisory Panel is established for the duration of the Review. That is, from the announcement of the Review until it concludes in July 2010. Process and operating procedures In general, the Advisory Panel will provide its advice through meetings with the Associate Minister. Between meetings the Associate Minister may seek collective or individual contributions via teleconference etc. Meetings and discussions may be attended by the Ministry of Education. Members will be required to have a good understanding of the current context for special education. Relevant research and reports will be provided. Etiquette Meetings will be conducted in a manner that promotes open and frank discussion. Discussion is confidential to those present Panel members will not disclose any information obtained as a member of the Panel. Any requests under the Official Information Act 1982 will be responded to by the Associate Minister and the Ministry through usual procedures. Meeting Procedure Meetings will be for no more than two half days a month and be arranged in advance and cognisant of members’ other priorities, of resources and in time for members to organise travel and other logistics. Documentation required for meetings should be provided to members at least five days ahead of time. Travel and expenses Members’ willingness to volunteer their time to be on the Panel is appreciated. Travel will be reimbursed by the Ministry on an actual and reasonable basis. Reporting date: Not applicable 3. The Inter-Party Working Group for School Choice National-ACT Confidence and Supply Agreement agreed to establish “an interparty Working Group to consider and report on policy options relating to the funding and regulation of schools that will increase parental choice and school autonomy.” Confidence and Supply Agreement dated 16 November, 2009.
